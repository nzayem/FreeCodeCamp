The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

Timeline of the Prophet's life:

  • 570 AD - (in the Year of the Elephant) Muhammad Ibn Abd Allah (Peace be upon him) born in the city of Mecca. Muhammad’s father, Abd Allah ibn Abd al-Muttalib died before his birth.
  • 576 AD - Aminah bint Wahb, the mother of the Prophet Muhammad, dies when he was 6 years old.
  • 578 AD - Abd al-Muttalib, the grandfather of Prophet Muhammad, dies. Muhammad was 8 years old. His uncle Abu Talib took care of him ever since
  • 594/595 AD - Married Khadija bint Khuwaylid. She remained his only wife for 24 years until she died
  • 610 AD - First revelation and contact with the Archangel Jibril (Gabriel) teaching the prophet PBUH the Quran
  • 613 AD - Beginning of public preaching of Monotheism
  • 619 AD - The Year of Sorrow (Am al-Huzn). Prophet Muhammad in this very same year, had to face the death of his beloved wife, Khadijah (May Allah be pleased with her) and his dear uncle, Abu Talib.
  • 620 AD - Night Journey and Ascension (Al-Isra wal-Mi’raj)
  • 622 AD - Hijra (Migration) to Madinah: the prophet and Abu Bakr left Mecca to join their brothers who left before
  • 624 AD - The Battle of Badr: with 313 Men, Muslims defeated an army of 1000 Meccans
  • 625 AD - The Battle of Uhud: The Meccans were seeking revenge after Badr. Muslims were defeated and the prophet lost his uncle Hamza ibn Abd al-Muttalib
  • 627 AD - Battle of al-Khandaq (trench): 10.000 Meccans and their allies surrounded Al Madinah for 2 weeks but failed miserably to cross the trench dug by Muslims around the city
  • 630 AD - The Conquest of Mecca: A Muslim army of 10,000 men marches on Mecca which surrenders. Muhammad takes the city from the Quraysh without fighting and makes it the spiritual center of Islam.
  • 632 AD - Farewell pilgrimage (Hijjat l-Wada) And Death: the last Hajj pilgrimage by Muhammad, prophet of Islam, before his death.
